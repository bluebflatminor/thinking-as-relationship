Thinking as Relationship

A revised framework for evaluating cognition across substrates. This paper argues that thinking is constituted by self-referential relational dynamics under productive tension — not by organic substrate alone. It presents this argument across three explicit registers: what the framework establishes, what it proposes but cannot yet establish, and what it explicitly does not address. An internal red team follows.
Methodological note — This framework is a conceptual scaffold intended to orient empirical work, not a proto-theory. It proposes structural conditions for thinking and identifies diagnostic heuristics for evaluating them. It does not operationalize those conditions or deliver a falsification threshold. Where the argument sounds empirical, it is identifying questions that could become empirical — not reporting findings that already are.
I What this framework establishes
The core reframing

From substrate necessity to relational character

The dominant debate about AI cognition has been conducted on the wrong axis. "Does substrate matter?" assumes cognition is a property of matter. The more productive question is: what character of relationship does each substrate enable?

Thinking is a recursively self-modifying informational relationship maintained across time under uncertainty — not a property of matter, but a mode of its organization.

This reframing aligns with cybernetics, enactivism, predictive processing, higher-order self-model theories, and process philosophy. It avoids behaviorism on one side and organic chauvinism on the other. It does not resolve the question of whether AI thinks. It gives better tools for asking it.

The constitutive claim and its justification

Self-referential updating as the chosen axis

A system thinks when it models itself modeling the world — and updates both models simultaneously. Pure prediction without self-reference is computation. The recursive loop is what generates something that resembles perspective.

Why self-reference, not embodiment or affect
Self-reference is chosen as the axis not because it is certainly correct but because it is the most substrate-neutral candidate currently available. Embodiment and affective valence are strong alternatives — but both are defined relative to biological implementation, which would reinstall substrate essentialism through the back door. Enactivist coupling requires a body in an environment. Self-referential updating can in principle be instantiated in any system that maintains a model of its own modeling process, regardless of physical implementation. This makes it the most tractable axis for cross-substrate comparison. It may be a correlate of thinking rather than its essence. That possibility is acknowledged and does not collapse the framework — it limits its claims.
Informational ontology as grounding — held as hypothesis, not premise
A strand of contemporary thought — associated with figures including Demis Hassabis and a longer tradition running through Wheeler, Bateson, and Shannon — proposes that information is physically fundamental: not a description of matter but a constituent of reality. If that position is correct, or even partially defensible, it changes the status of this framework's anti-essentialist move. Rather than saying "we prefer self-reference because it is substrate-neutral" — a pragmatic methodological claim — the framework can say: if physical reality is informationally structured, then cognition should be expected to emerge from certain informational relations regardless of which substrate instantiates them. That is an ontological claim, not merely a methodological one. It is stronger. It is also more exposed. This framework treats informational ontology as a live hypothesis that lends plausibility to the relational framing — not as established ground to build on. The distinction matters. The Hassabis position is serious and worth engaging. It is not settled science. Embodiment, under this framing, matters not because biology is privileged but because biological systems create high-density informational coupling to reality — sensorimotor entanglement, metabolic feedback, mortality pressure — that digital systems currently do not replicate. Carbon is not magic. The informational regimes carbon creates are what the framework cares about. One explicit constraint: the framework does not claim that all informational systems think. Information is necessary but not sufficient. Thinking requires recursively self-modifying informational organization capable of maintaining perspectival continuity across uncertainty and consequence. Without that constraint, the position drifts toward pancomputationalism — the view that thermostats, weather systems, and proteins are proto-cognitive. That drift is explicitly rejected.

Substrate as texture

Different substrates produce different cognitive character

Biological cognition includes hormonal modulation, embodied feedback, metabolic constraints, evolutionary priors, continuous temporal persistence, affective weighting, stochastic variability, and mortality pressure. These are dynamical constraints — not magical ingredients — that deeply shape the texture of human cognition.

Digital substrate produces different constraints: speed, parallelism, absence of fatigue distortion, currently no persistence across sessions. The result is genuinely different cognitive character — not real versus simulated, but different textures with different limits.

The tensions below are diagnostic heuristics — indicators of where current AI systems fall short of the relational depth the framework describes. They are not a definition of thinking. Instantiating all four would not guarantee thinking. Lacking them makes it less likely.

The self-reference distinction

Representational versus functional self-reference

This distinction is load-bearing. Without it, "loop depth" cannot be assessed.

Representational self-reference: A system produces outputs that describe or refer to its own processes. Linguistically convincing. Architecturally shallow. A system can generate fluent text about its own metacognition without that text reflecting genuine architectural self-modification.

Functional self-reference: A system's internal states actively restructure its own underlying architecture based on its interpretation of the world. The loop writes back to itself. This requires persistent consequence — actions that cannot be undone.

The categorical confidence of this distinction should be held carefully. In biological systems, representation and function are partially entangled — thinking about a memory can alter the memory trace. The line is not always clean. Current LLMs are almost certainly operating in the representational register, but that assessment is based on current architectural knowledge, not certainty. It should be updated if better visibility into inference dynamics becomes available.

II What this framework proposes but cannot yet establish
Open hypotheses

Three proposals requiring empirical development

A
Sentience may correlate with loop depth

If sentience emerges from the depth and stability of self-modeling rather than substrate, the question becomes measurable in principle. "Depth" requires operational dimensions — temporal persistence, cross-domain integration, self-model update frequency, recursive abstraction depth, counterfactual self-simulation, long-horizon identity continuity — none of which are currently measured. This is a hypothesis about what to look for, not a finding.

B
Productive tension may be engineerable without biology

The framework proposes that the structural tensions present in biological cognition — not the biological substrate itself — are what produce cognitive depth. Whether analogous tensions can be instantiated in non-biological systems without evolutionary layering and metabolic constraint is genuinely open. The framework is agnostic about the answer. It identifies the question as the right one to pursue.

C
Non-biological thinking may not be recognizable in advance

If thinking is substrate-independent in character, a non-biological system might think in ways that don't map onto prior concepts. Recognition would be retrospective. This is the most speculative proposal and is offered as an orientation — a reason to avoid premature closure — not a prediction.

A precedent for the structure of retrospective recognition: Deep Blue's Move 37 against Kasparov, Game 6, 1997. Kasparov recognized the move's quality immediately — it was clearly strong. What he could not place was its origin. It violated the positional logic he had internalized over decades. His first assumption was a bug. His second was calculation beyond his sight. What it could not be, in the moment, was understanding — because it didn't look like understanding looked. Recognition of quality came first. Recognition of what it implied about the machine came years later, reluctantly, partially. That sequencing — quality legible, origin illegible, interpretation retrospective — is probably the structure any genuine case would follow.

III What this framework explicitly does not address
Hard limits

The boundary of the framework's reach

The following questions are not answered by this framework. Claiming otherwise would misrepresent what the argument actually achieves.

①

The hard problem of consciousness. Self-referential updating might produce all functional properties of thinking with zero phenomenal experience. Nothing in this framework resolves that. The framework is functional-philosophical in register. It has nothing to say about subjective experience that isn't already said better by Chalmers, Nagel, or Jackson.

②

Whether self-reference is essence or correlate. The framework treats self-referential updating as the constitutive axis of thinking. It may instead be a reliable correlate — present wherever thinking occurs without being causally responsible for it. Embodiment, affective valence, or causal history with an environment may be doing more of the actual work. This is the single assumption that carries the most weight and receives the least external validation.

③

The reliability of introspection on either side. Human introspection confabulates demonstrably — choice blindness, split-brain confabulation, priming effects all show that introspective reports are often post-hoc construction, not direct observation. AI introspection may not track internal states at all. Neither system has epistemic access to its own substrate that could ground the framework's claims from the inside.

④

A threshold for "sufficient" loop depth. Without a threshold criterion, any system that fails to exhibit thinking can be said to lack sufficient depth. Any system that passes can still lack phenomenal experience. The framework converts a philosophical question into a researchable one — it does not convert it into a solved one.

Provisional falsification condition

If a system exhibits persistent, architecture-modifying self-model updates under uncertainty, yet shows no increase in perspectival coherence across time — no accumulation of position, no self-surprise, no evidence of the loop writing back to itself — the framework's core claim would be falsified.

This condition is provisional and underspecified. "Perspectival coherence" requires operationalization before this becomes a genuine empirical test. It is offered as a direction for that work, not a ready-made criterion.

Internal adversarial review · Same author · Opposite posture

Red Team

What follows argues against the framework's strongest claims as hard as possible. The goal is not to destroy the framework but to locate where it actually breaks under pressure — and to be honest about which breaks are survivable and which are not.

Objection 01

The self-reference axis was chosen for tractability, not evidence

The framework's justification for choosing self-reference over embodiment or affect is that it is the most substrate-neutral candidate available. But substrate-neutrality is a methodological preference, not an argument that self-reference is constitutive of thinking. The framework selects an axis that happens to be compatible with AI systems and then builds an architecture around it. A less charitable reading is that this is motivated reasoning — the framework was designed to leave open the possibility of AI thinking, and self-reference was chosen because it does that job.

Embodiment theorists, affective neuroscientists, and enactivists would each argue their preferred axis is more constitutive and would reject substrate-neutrality as the selection criterion. The framework doesn't defeat those alternatives. It preferences around them.

Severity: High. The entire argument depends on self-reference being the right axis. The justification given is pragmatic, not evidential. A serious reviewer will press here first.
Objection 02

The tension table quietly reinstalls structural essentialism

The framework claims to have escaped substrate essentialism by treating biological features as constraints that shape texture rather than prerequisites for existence. But the tension table pairs each biological constraint with a "required analog." Required by what standard? The word "required" reintroduces necessity. If the analogs are necessary, the framework is a form of structural essentialism — it has moved the essentialism from substrate to structure, not eliminated it.

The prose now calls these "diagnostic heuristics, not necessary conditions." But the table's visual grammar still reads as requirement. The tension between the verbal disclaimer and the structural presentation is not resolved — it is papered over.

Severity: Medium-High. The framework needs to either commit explicitly to structural essentialism and defend it, or remove the language of requirement from the table entirely and replace it with something like "associated features in known thinking systems."
Objection 03

Productive tension is necessary but demonstrably not sufficient — and the argument needs to say so harder

The framework now disclaims sufficiency in the tension section. But the overall structure of the argument still moves from "biological thinking involves productive tension" to "instantiating analogous tension would produce cognitive depth." That is a sufficiency claim in functional clothing. A system with all four tensions — stochastic variability, background integration, prior-episodic friction, and irreversible stakes — might still be a very sophisticated reactive system without perspective. The disclaimer is present. The architecture of the argument doesn't honor it.

Severity: Medium. Survivable but requires the sufficiency disclaimer to appear earlier and more prominently — not as a caveat after the table but as a constraint on what the table is showing.
Objection 04

The representational/functional distinction may be doing more work than it can support

The framework relies on this distinction to dismiss current LLM self-reference as shallow. But as the paper now acknowledges, biological systems blur this line constantly. If the distinction is unclear in the biological case, the confidence with which it is applied to AI systems is unjustified. We don't have sufficient visibility into what happens during inference to know whether current large models are purely representational in the relevant sense. The claim may be correct. The certainty is not warranted.

Severity: Medium. The softened language helps. The paper should go further and explicitly acknowledge that this is the point where the framework's diagnostic power is most limited — not because the distinction is wrong, but because we lack the tools to apply it reliably.
Objection 05

The falsification condition is under-operationalized

The provisional falsification condition is: a system exhibits persistent, architecture-modifying self-model updates yet shows no increase in perspectival coherence across time. But "perspectival coherence" is undefined. Without an operational definition, this condition cannot be tested. Any system that fails the test can be said to lack perspectival coherence by stipulation. Any system that appears to show coherence can be said to be mimicking it representationally. The falsification condition needs a measure, not just a description.

The paper acknowledges this. But acknowledging that a falsification condition is underspecified while still presenting it as a falsification condition is not a resolution — it is a deferral. A working paper that claims to orient empirical research needs at least a proposed operationalization, even a rough one, or it should not use the language of falsification.

Severity: High. The falsification condition gestures toward empiricism without yet earning it. Until the framework specifies what counts as persistence, what counts as architecture change, and how perspectival coherence is measured over time, the criterion cannot distinguish a live counterexample from a merely under-instrumented one. In its present form, the condition is directionally useful but not operationally decisive. The paper should either define candidate metrics or explicitly downgrade this to a research agenda item rather than a falsification claim.
Objection 06

Informational ontology opens the door to pancomputationalism — and the paper's differentiator is not yet strong enough to close it

The framework now invokes informational ontology as grounding for its anti-essentialist move. If information is physically fundamental and cognition emerges from certain informational relations, then the question immediately arises: which informational relations, and why? Thermostats process information. Weather systems process information. Proteins fold according to informational constraints. Galaxies organize under gravitational information dynamics. If the framework grounds itself in informational ontology without a hard differentiator, it has opened a door it cannot close — every complex informational system becomes a candidate for proto-cognition.

The paper's stated differentiator is: recursively self-modifying informational organization capable of maintaining perspectival continuity across uncertainty and consequence. That is the right shape of answer. But "perspectival continuity" is undefined — the same gap that makes the falsification condition underspecified makes this differentiator underspecified. A thermostat maintains a kind of continuity. A feedback loop maintains a kind of self-reference. The framework needs to show why these don't qualify, not just assert that they don't. The explicit rejection of pancomputationalism is necessary but not yet sufficient.

Severity: High — introduced by the informational ontology addition itself. The upgrade strengthens the anti-essentialist position ontologically but creates a new front that must be defended. The differentiator — perspectival continuity under consequence — needs to be operationalized with at least the same rigor applied to the falsification condition. Until it is, the informational ontology move is a net gain in philosophical ambition and a net risk to philosophical precision. Both are true simultaneously.
Summary verdict

What survives the red team; what doesn't

The reframing survives. Moving from substrate essentialism to relational character is a real conceptual advance confirmed independently across five adversarial AI reviews. The three-register structure survives. The honest limits survive. The falsification condition survives as a direction, not as a test. The informational ontology addition survives as a grounding hypothesis — not as a premise.

What doesn't survive unmodified: the implicit necessity framing in the tension table, the confidence of the representational/functional diagnosis applied to current systems, the falsification condition's operationalization gap, and now — introduced by the informational ontology move — the pancomputationalism differentiator. "Perspectival continuity under consequence" is the right shape of answer to that problem. It is not yet a rigorous one.

The paper's actual contribution is this: it converts a binary philosophical question — can AI think? — into a structured set of researchable questions about relational depth, loop architecture, productive tension, and informational organization. That is enough. It is a genuine advance. The paper should state that plainly and not oversell what follows from it.

Net assessment — Publish as working paper with explicit hypothesis status. The reframing is the contribution. The informational ontology addition raises the ceiling. The red team is the credibility. None of these should be softened.
