# Thinking as Relationship
### A Working Framework on Cognition Across Substrates

**Solbakken Research Initiative · N. Haaland · May 2026**
*Not peer reviewed · Developed in dialogue with Claude (Anthropic)*

---

## What this is

A conceptual scaffold for evaluating cognition across substrates — biological and artificial. The central argument: thinking is not a property of matter but a mode of its organization. Specifically, a recursively self-modifying informational relationship maintained across time under uncertainty.

This is a working paper, not a settled theory. It proposes structural conditions for thinking and identifies diagnostic heuristics for evaluating them. It does not operationalize those conditions or deliver a falsification threshold. Where the argument sounds empirical, it is identifying questions that could become empirical — not reporting findings that already are.

---

## What it argues

The paper is structured across three explicit registers:

**I — What the framework establishes**
The move from substrate essentialism to relational character. Thinking assessed by the nature of the loop, not the material of the system. Self-referential updating chosen as the axis — substrate-neutral, tractable, acknowledged as possibly correlate rather than essence. Informational ontology introduced as grounding hypothesis, not premise. Four productive tensions identified as diagnostic heuristics. Representational vs. functional self-reference distinguished, with explicit acknowledgment that the distinction requires timescale differentiation the paper does not yet provide.

**II — What it proposes but cannot yet establish**
Three open hypotheses: sentience may correlate with loop depth; productive tension may be engineerable without biology; non-biological thinking may not be recognizable in advance. The Deep Blue / Kasparov Move 37 case cited as structural precedent for retrospective recognition — quality legible, origin illegible, interpretation retrospective.

**III — What it explicitly does not address**
The hard problem of consciousness. Whether self-reference is essence or correlate. The reliability of introspection on either side. A threshold for sufficient loop depth. A provisional falsification condition is offered with explicit acknowledgment that it is not yet operationally decisive.

## Internal red team

The paper contains a six-objection internal adversarial review arguing against its own strongest claims:

1. Self-reference axis chosen for tractability, not evidence
2. Tension table quietly reinstalls structural essentialism
3. Productive tension necessary but not sufficient — argument undersells this
4. Representational/functional distinction doing more work than it can support
5. Falsification condition under-operationalized — gestures toward empiricism without earning it
6. Informational ontology opens pancomputationalism — differentiator not yet rigorous enough

Objections 05 and 06 are unresolved. They are named as such.

---

## Known gaps — next document

Six independent adversarial reviews converged on three problems that are real and not addressed in this version:

- **Perspectival continuity** is the paper's most central undefined term. It appears at the constitutive claim, the falsification condition, and the pancomputationalism differentiator. It requires operationalization before the framework becomes an empirical research program rather than a philosophical scaffold.
- **Timescale differentiation** for the representational/functional self-reference distinction. Seconds, minutes, developmental — these are different claims. Human working memory doesn't permanently modify synaptic weights either. The distinction as currently drawn may be too strict or too coarse.
- **Existential vs. informational irreversibility** in Tension 04. A system that can be rebooted but develops preferences about its own continuity may be closer to thinking than one with no reboot available. The binary treatment needs refinement.

These are the starting points for the next document, not corrections to this one.

**What Paper 2 does with Paper 1's open problems:**

Paper 1 identified perspectival continuity as its most central undefined term — present in three critical locations, operationalized in none. Paper 2 proposes a physics-grounded operationalization:

- **C1 — Structural irreversibility:** computation alters the underlying physical substrate permanently
- **C2 — State-dependent feedback:** current state Ψ(t) is a continuous function of all previous states

Both must operate simultaneously in the same high-dimensional representational space. This discriminates against thermostats and delay lines while giving hybrid resonant architectures a theoretical path toward satisfying the conditions. Whether C1 and C2 are sufficient as well as necessary for perspectival continuity is Paper 3's problem.
